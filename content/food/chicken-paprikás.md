## Chicken paprikás, a family favorite.
One of my favorite meals growing up was my Grandma Jean's chicken paprikás. She made it at least several times a year for us while I was growing up. Recently my wife Sara and I have been going to her house once a week to visit and make her dinner. This week it was our turn to make this dish for her.

![Finished Paprikás](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__finished-paprikas.jpg)

## The prep
The stock, dumplings, and chicken brine, were completed the day before visiting Grandma.

### Chicken stock
This recipe is from scratch, including the stock.

![Vegetables as part of the stock base.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__stock-vegetables.jpg)

After breaking down the chicken into skin-on legs, wings and breasts, I browned the chicken scraps by roasting them in the oven before adding them to simmer in the stockpot.

![Browned chicken scraps before they joined the stockpot.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__roasted-scraps.jpg)

Liquid chicken gold.

![Liquid chicken gold.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__chicken-stock.jpg)

### Brining the chicken pieces
One key to extra moist and flavorful chicken is a 6 hour brine.

![Chicken pieces in the brine.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__brining.jpg)

### The dumplings
Because they are time-consuming, I also made the dumplings the day before.

![The wet and dry of the dumplings](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__dumplings-wet-dry.jpg)

Use the "well" technique.

![How to start the dumplings](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__dumplings-start.gif)

We have always made them larger than German spaetzle, and slightly larger than traditional Nokedli.

![Forming the dumplings](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__dumplings-forming.gif)

![Bowl of dumplings](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__dumplings-bowl.jpg)

![Sara with the finished goods.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__sara-finished.jpg)

## Bringing it all together
On the day of the dinner we fried and simmered the chicken and prepared the most important part, the sauce.

![Starting the sauce.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__making-sauce.jpg)

Render a whole pound of bacon fat, keep the crispy bacon for a topping.

![Rendering the fat out of the bacon.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__rendering-bacon.jpg)

Bread the chicken with a mixture of flour, paprika, and salt to taste.

![Bread the chicken](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__bread-chicken.jpg)

Brown the chicken to add flavor, no need to cook thoroughly.

![Brown the chicken](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__brown-chicken.jpg)

![Fry the chicken in the bacon grease](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__fry-chicken.jpg)

Now simmer the onions in the remaining grease just until translucent.

![Onions simmering](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__onions-simmering.gif){: width=40% }

![then soften the onions in the remaining bacon grease](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__soften-onions.jpg)

Layer the onions in with the chicken.

![Layer in chicken with onions.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__layer-chicken-onions.jpg)

Now add with chicken stock concentrate.

![Add the chicken stock](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__add-stock.jpg)

Simmer long enough to finish cooking the chicken and completely disintegrate the onions.

![bubbling away](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__bubbling.gif)

The finished product. To finish the sauce, add paprika and sour cream to taste. You can also thicken with flour if needed.

![The finished product](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__finished-product.jpg)

We all enjoyed it.

![We all enjoyed it.](../img/2019-10-09__chicken-paprikas__all-enjoyed.jpg)
